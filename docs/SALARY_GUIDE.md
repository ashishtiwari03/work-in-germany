## 💶 Brutto vs. Netto — The Painful Truth

In Germany, your **Brutto (gross) salary** and your **Netto (net/take-home) salary** are VERY different. Expect to lose **35-45%** of your gross salary to taxes and social contributions.

### What Gets Deducted

| Deduction | Approximate % | What it covers |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Income Tax (Lohnsteuer) | 14-42% (progressive) | Federal + state income tax |
| Solidarity Surcharge | 0-5.5% of tax | (mostly eliminated, only for high earners) |
| Church Tax (Kirchensteuer) | 8-9% of tax | **Only if registered with a church** — deregister to save ~€100-300/month |
| Health Insurance | ~7.3% | Your half of public health insurance |
| Pension (Rentenversicherung) | ~9.3% | State pension — you get this back when you retire (even abroad) |
| Unemployment Insurance | ~1.3% | Arbeitslosenversicherung |
| Nursing Care Insurance | ~1.7-2.3% | Pflegeversicherung — higher if childless and over 23 |

### Quick Netto Estimator

|------------------------|-----------------------|---------------------------|
| €40,000 | ~€2,150 | Entry-level |
| €50,000 | ~€2,600 | Junior developer |
| €60,000 | ~€3,050 | Mid-level |

### Tax Classes (Steuerklassen)

| Class | Who | Notes |
|-------|-----|-------|
| I | Single, no children | Default for most |
| III | Married, higher earner | Much less tax — combined with V |
| IV | Married, both earn similarly | Equal split |
| V | Married, lower earner | More tax — combined with III |

**Tip for couples:** If one partner earns significantly more, Class III/V combination saves a lot of tax monthly (though you settle up at year-end filing).

## 🤝 Negotiation Tips for Germany

German salary negotiation is more conservative than the US, but there IS room to negotiate.

### What to negotiate
- **Base salary** — the most important number
- **Signing bonus** — less common in Germany but worth asking at larger companies
- **Remote work days** — very negotiable (2-3 days/week is standard)
- **Vacation days** — legal minimum is 20, most companies offer 28-30. Push for 30.
- **Training budget** — €1,000-3,000/year for conferences, courses
- **Public transport ticket** — Deutschlandticket (€49/month) — many employers pay this
- **Company pension** (Betriebliche Altersvorsorge) — employer contribution on top of salary
- **Relocation package** — if moving to Germany: flights, temporary housing, German course

### What NOT to negotiate (usually fixed)
- Health insurance contributions (legally mandated)
- Pension contributions (legally mandated)
- Working hours (usually 38-40h/week, defined by contract/Tarifvertrag)

### How to State Your Expectation
When asked for your salary expectation (Gehaltsvorstellung), always state:
- **Annual gross (Brutto Jahresgehalt)**
- Example: "My salary expectation is €65,000 Brutto per year"
- Don't mention monthly, don't mention net

### Helpful phrase
> "Meine Gehaltsvorstellung liegt bei €XX.XXX brutto im Jahr."

## 🎁 Common Benefits in Germany

| Benefit | How common | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| 28-30 vacation days | Very common | Legal minimum is 20. Tech usually offers 28-30 |
| Flexible working hours (Gleitzeit) | Very common | Core hours + flexible start/end |
| Remote/hybrid work | Common in tech | Usually 2-3 days WFH |
| Deutschlandticket / BVG ticket | Common | €49/month public transit — employer often pays |
| Company pension (bAV) | Common | Employer adds ~€50-200/month to your pension |
| Training/conference budget | Common | €1,000-5,000/year |
| Gym membership / Urban Sports Club | Fairly common | Especially at startups |
| Meal vouchers (Essenszuschuss) | Fairly common | €6-8/day tax-free |
| Sabbatical option | Some companies | After 2-5 years, unpaid leave guaranteed |
| Kindergarten subsidy | Growing | Tax-free childcare support |
| 13th month salary | Traditional companies | Especially with Tarifvertrag |

## 📖 Key Terms

| German | English | Why it matters |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| Brutto | Gross salary | What you negotiate |
| Netto | Net/take-home | What hits your bank |
| Tarifvertrag | Collective agreement | Fixed salary bands — no individual negotiation |
| Probezeit | Probation period | Usually 6 months — shorter notice period during this |
| Kündigungsfrist | Notice period | Usually 3 months after Probezeit — check your contract |
| Arbeitszeugnis | Employment reference | Every employer MUST give you one by law when you leave |
| Betriebsrat | Works council | Employee representation — cannot be fired easily |
| Überstunden | Overtime | Check if your contract says "mit dem Gehalt abgegolten" (included in salary) |
